  <p><strong>1. CULTURAL & TOURISM APPLICATIONS</strong></p>
  <p>
    Node 01 functions as both material system and geographic record. Each pigment batch encodes local botanical inputs, positioning the output as “Terroir-based media” tied to specific landscapes and ecological conditions.
  </p>

  <p>
    - Terroir Pigment Line: pigments derived from local invasive species (e.g., knapweed) or agricultural byproducts (e.g., grape, cherry skins)<br>
    - Workshop-as-a-Service: guided “field-to-fiber” production experiences where participants harvest botanical inputs and produce finished ink sticks<br>
    - Forensic Branding: emphasis on process transparency and laboratory-style presentation elevates product positioning from craft to bio-material technology
  </p>

  <p><strong>2. PRODUCT EMBODIMENT: THE SOVEREIGN STICK</strong></p>

  <p>
    The primary commercial unit is a solid watercolor/ink stick designed for portability, long-term storage, and low-waste distribution.
  </p>

  <p><strong>2.1 Shelf Life & Stability</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Unused (solid state): 3–5 years due to low water activity and chitosan-based antimicrobial stability<br>
    - In-use state: effectively indefinite if properly dried between uses<br>
    - Chromatic Integrity: 12–24 months peak saturation for organic dyes, followed by gradual softening depending on storage conditions
  </p>

  <p><strong>3. MICRO-BUSINESS REVENUE MODEL (3-PERSON OPERATION)</strong></p>

  <p><strong>3.1 Roles</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Lead Technician: manages biological ramp and extraction systems<br>
    - Formulator: handles mulling, molding, and quality control<br>
    - Liaison/Admin: oversees outreach, packaging, and distribution
  </p>

  <p><strong>3.2 Unit Economics (Sample Batch: 100 Sticks)</strong></p>

  <p>

    - Packaging: $80 (recycled materials and branded wraps)<br>
