  <p><strong>1. SUBSTRATE REDUCTION (MICRONIZATION)</strong></p>
  <p>
    Botanical substrate must be reduced to high surface-area particulate form to ensure complete enzymatic access and pigment liberation. Processing method varies by operational environment.
  </p>

  <p><strong>1.1 Tier I: Forensic Lab Standard</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Target Particle Size: &lt;100 μm<br>
    - Mechanism: planetary ball mill with zirconia media<br>
    - Protocol: 30-minute dry milling cycle at 400 RPM<br>
    - Verification: full pass through 140-mesh (105 μm) laboratory sieve<br>
    - Benefit: maximum enzymatic yield and high-fidelity pigment suspension
  </p>

  <p><strong>1.2 Tier II: In-Field / Expedient Solution</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Target Particle Size: &lt;300 μm<br>
    - Mechanism: ceramic burr grinder or granite mortar and pestle<br>
    - Protocol: dual-pass grinding followed by 60-mesh manual sifting<br>
    - Adjustment: increase fermentation time by +12 hours to compensate for reduced surface area<br>
    - Benefit: operational sovereignty in off-grid environments
  </p>

  <p><strong>2. THE BIOLOGICAL RAMP (ENZYMATIC LIBERATION)</strong></p>

  <p><strong>2.1 Inoculation & Incubation</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Catalyst: <em>Aspergillus oryzae</em> (Koji spores)<br>
    - Dosage: 1.0 g spores per 1.0 kg hydrated substrate<br>
    - Tier I (Lab): 30°C (86°F), 90% RH, controlled chamber incubation for 48 hours<br>
    - Tier II (Field): ambient insulated incubation (thermal containment), 60–72 hours
  </p>

  <p><strong>3. EXTRACTION & PURITY STANDARDS</strong></p>

  <p><strong>3.1 Filtration</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Tier I (Lab): vacuum filtration through 5-micron Buchner funnel system<br>
    - Tier II (Field): gravity filtration cascade<br>
    &nbsp;&nbsp;• 100-mesh screen<br>
    &nbsp;&nbsp;• Grade 1 coffee filter<br>
    &nbsp;&nbsp;• silk or nylon mesh (50–100 μm)
  </p>

  <p><strong>4.1 Sovereign Binder Matrix (w/w ratios)</strong></p>
