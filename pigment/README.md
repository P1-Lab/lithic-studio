  <p><strong>Node 01: The Light (Botanical Pigment Extraction)</strong></p>

  <p>
    <strong>Industrial Classification:</strong> LITH-LIGHT-01<br>
    <strong>Status:</strong> Forensic Specification / Stand-Alone Medium<br>
    <strong>Core Engine:</strong> Enzymatic Vacuole Liberation & Solvent Concentration
  </p>

  <p><strong>Overview</strong></p>
  <p>
    Node 01: The Light is a forensic protocol for the enzymatic liberation of chromatic data from regional botanical matter. The system defines a complete vertical stack for producing professional-grade artist media, including high-density pigment pastes and solid watercolor/ink sticks.
  </p>

  <p>
    While chemically compatible with the Lithic Fresco system, Node 01 is designed as a fully stand-alone production node, enabling conversion of botanical waste into stabilized pigment media without reliance on industrial pigment supply chains.
  </p>

  <p><strong>Project Scope</strong></p>

  <p>
    - Enzymatic Pre-treatment: controlled inoculation of botanical substrates for cell-wall breakdown via <em>Aspergillus oryzae</em><br>
    - Stand-Alone Formulation: precise compounding of extracted pigments into solid watercolor and ink sticks using organic binders (gum arabic, glycerin)<br>
    - Bimodal Extraction: polar (aqueous) and non-polar (solvent-based) chromophore isolation protocols<br>
    - Refinement & Concentration: vacuum filtration and thermal reduction to achieve industrial-grade viscosity and pigment density
  </p>

  <p><strong>Technical Features</strong></p>

  <p>
    <strong>Sovereign Media</strong><br>
    Produces a finished, shelf-stable pigment system directly from raw botanical inputs, eliminating dependency on external pigment manufacturing.
  </p>

  <p>
    <strong>High-Purity Chromophores</strong><br>
    Selectively isolates color compounds while rejecting starches and non-chromatic organics, improving transparency and tinting strength.
  </p>

  <p>
    <strong>Vertical Integration (Optional)</strong><br>
    Optimized compatibility with Lithic Substrate (Node 03) via chitosan sizing for enhanced edge retention and binding stability.
  </p>

  <p><strong>System Architecture</strong></p>

  <p>
    Node 01 functions as one of three independent pillars within the Lithic Studio ecosystem:
  </p>

  <p>
    - Node 01 (The Light): pigment and ink production system<br>
    - Node 02 (The Form): structural fresco and impasto media system<br>
    - Node 03 (The Body): botanical substrate and paper fabrication system
  </p>
