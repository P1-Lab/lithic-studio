  <p><strong>EXTRACTION PROTOCOLS: Node 01 (The Light)</strong></p>

  <p>
    <strong>Industrial Classification:</strong> LITH-LIGHT-PROT-01<br>
    <strong>Status:</strong> VERIFIED<br>
    <strong>Process Type:</strong> Bimodal Solvent Extraction & Thermal Concentration
  </p>

  <p><strong>1. PRE-EXTRACTION AUDIT</strong></p>
  <p>
    Extraction begins only after completion of the Biological Ramp (TECHNICAL_SPECIFICATIONS.md). The fermented substrate (“Koji-cake”) must exhibit full mycelial saturation, indicating successful enzymatic breakdown of botanical cell structure and readiness for chromophore liberation.
  </p>

  <p><strong>2. PROTOCOL A: POLAR (AQUEOUS) EXTRACTION</strong></p>
  <p>
    <strong>Target Chromophores:</strong> Betalains, Anthocyanins (e.g., beetroot, hibiscus, red cabbage, black carrot)
  </p>

  <p><strong>2.1 Methodology</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Solvent: distilled water (pH adjusted to ~4.5 using citric acid for anthocyanin stability)<br>
    - Ratio: 3:1 solvent to fermented substrate (w/w)<br>
    - Thermal Ramp: 80°C (176°F), maintained for 120 minutes under low-shear agitation<br>
    - Tier I (Lab): magnetic stirrer / hot plate system<br>
    - Tier II (Field): controlled double-boiler heat source system
  </p>

  <p><strong>3. PROTOCOL B: NON-POLAR (SOLVENT) EXTRACTION</strong></p>
  <p>
    <strong>Target Chromophores:</strong> Curcuminoids, carotenoids (e.g., turmeric, annatto, marigold, saffron)
  </p>

  <p><strong>3.1 Methodology</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Solvent: 95% ethanol<br>
    - Ratio: 2:1 solvent to dehydrated fermented substrate (w/w)<br>
    - Moisture Constraint: substrate must be &lt;5% moisture prior to solvent exposure<br>
    - Thermal Ramp: 60°C (140°F); must not exceed 70°C due to solvent volatility risk<br>
    - Tier I (Lab): sealed reflux apparatus for solvent retention and efficiency<br>
    - Tier II (Field): sealed glass vessel with indirect warm-water bath (solar or residual heat source)
  </p>

  <p><strong>4. FILTRATION & REFINEMENT</strong></p>

  <p>
    The objective is removal of all suspended solids to achieve high-fidelity chromatic clarity.
  </p>

  <p>
    - Stage 1 (Coarse): 100-mesh stainless steel filtration<br>
    - Stage 2 (Fine): 20-micron filtration<br>
    - Stage 3 (Forensic Refinement):<br>
    &nbsp;&nbsp;• Tier I: vacuum Buchner filtration (5-micron)<br>
    &nbsp;&nbsp;• Tier II: gravity filtration through silk or Grade 1 laboratory paper
  </p>

  <p><strong>5. THERMAL CONCENTRATION (REDUCTION PHASE)</strong></p>

  <p>
    Solvent removal is performed without thermal degradation of chromophores.
  </p>

  <p>
    - Vessel: shallow glass or stainless steel evaporation basin<br>
    - Temperature: maintained at 50°C (122°F)<br>
    - Tier I (Lab): rotary evaporator for controlled solvent recovery<br>
    - Tier II (Field): shallow pan evaporation with forced airflow in dust-controlled environment<br>
    - Termination Point: viscosity >5,000 cP (honey-like consistency)
  </p>

  <p><strong>6. FINAL STABILIZATION</strong></p>

  <p>
    - Anhydrous Check: if residual moisture is present in non-polar extract, secondary drying or centrifugation (“salting out”) may be applied<br>
    - Output: stabilized pigment concentrate ready for stand-alone ink formulation or Lithic Fresco integration
  </p>
