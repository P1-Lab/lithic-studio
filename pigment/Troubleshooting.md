  <p><strong>TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE: Node 01 (The Light)</strong></p>

  <p>
    <strong>Industrial Classification:</strong> LITH-LIGHT-TS-01<br>
    <strong>Status:</strong> VERIFIED<br>
    <strong>Scope:</strong> Biological, Chemical, and Mechanical Failure Recovery
  </p>

  <p><strong>1. BIOLOGICAL RAMP FAILURES (FERMENTATION)</strong></p>

  <p><strong>1.1 Problem: No Mycelial Growth (The “Dead Cake”)</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Symptom: no visible white mycelium after 48 hours<br>
    - Root Cause: inoculation temperature too high (&gt;35°C) or substrate too dry<br>
    - Correction: ensure substrate is &lt;35°C (95°F) before inoculation; maintain ~90% relative humidity<br>
    - Recovery: re-sterilize at 100°C, re-hydrate, and re-inoculate with fresh spores
  </p>

  <p><strong>1.2 Problem: Foul Odor or Non-White Mold Growth</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Symptom: putrid odor or green/black contamination<br>
    - Root Cause: bacterial contamination or wild Aspergillus species due to inadequate sterilization<br>
    - Correction: enforce full sterilization cycle and sanitize vessels with 70% ethanol<br>
    - Recovery: TERMINATE BATCH; do not attempt salvage due to cross-contamination risk
  </p>

  <p><strong>2. EXTRACTION & CHROMATIC FAILURES</strong></p>

  <p><strong>2.1 Problem: Weak Color Yield (Dull Extract)</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Symptom: low saturation or pale pigment output<br>
    - Root Cause: incomplete fermentation or incorrect solvent polarity selection<br>
    - Correction: verify solvent compatibility (water vs ethanol system requirements)<br>
    - Recovery: re-grind fermented substrate to finer particle size and re-extract using correct solvent protocol
  </p>

  <p><strong>2.2 Problem: Browning / Color Shift</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Symptom: red to brown or yellow dulling<br>
    - Root Cause: thermal degradation (>80°C) or oxidative enzymatic browning<br>
    - Correction: maintain strict 50°C thermal ceiling during concentration phase<br>
    - Recovery: add 0.5% citric acid for pH stabilization; thermal damage is irreversible
  </p>

  <p><strong>3. FORMULATION FAILURES (INK STICK)</strong></p>

  <p><strong>3.1 Problem: Brittle or Crumbling Stick</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Symptom: fracture under light handling pressure<br>
    - Root Cause: insufficient humectant (glycerin/honey) or excessive pigment loading<br>
    - Correction: increase glycerin by ~2% in next formulation cycle<br>
    - Recovery: re-crush material, add 5% warm gum arabic solution + 2% glycerin, re-mull and reform
  </p>

  <p><strong>3.2 Problem: Stick Does Not Dry (Tacky State)</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Symptom: persistent softness or surface tack after 72 hours<br>
    - Root Cause: high humidity or excessive plasticizer content<br>
    - Correction: use forced-air drying at 35°C; reduce humectant levels in future batches<br>
    - Recovery: desiccant chamber treatment with silica gel for 48 hours
  </p>

  <p><strong>4. APPLICATION FAILURES (SUBSTRATE INTERACTION)</strong></p>

  <p><strong>4.1 Problem: Haloing (Uncontrolled Bleed)</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Symptom: pigment spreads beyond intended stroke boundary<br>
    - Root Cause: insufficient substrate sizing (Node 03 chitosan layer inadequate)<br>
    - Correction: apply additional 0.5% chitosan sizing layer to substrate<br>
    - Recovery: no correction possible post-application; treat remaining substrate before continued use
  </p>

  <p><strong>4.2 Problem: Grittiness During Application</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Symptom: abrasive or sandy texture during rewetting<br>
    - Root Cause: inadequate micronization or incomplete mulling<br>
    - Correction: verify full passage through <100 μm threshold; extend mulling cycle to 30 minutes<br>
    - Recovery: reprocess through planetary ball mill to restore particle uniformity
  </p>

  <p><strong>A=A.</strong> Failure is data; the protocol is the correction.</p>
