  <p><strong>1. LABORATORY SAFETY & HAZARD MITIGATION</strong></p>
  <p>
    Pigment production involves biological catalysts and volatile solvents. Safety constraints are mandatory across both Tier I and Tier II operational environments.
  </p>

  <p><strong>1.1 Ethanol Extraction Hazards (Protocol B)</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Flash Point: 95% ethanol ~13°C (55°F)<br>
    - Thermal Constraint: must not exceed 70°C (158°F)<br>
    - Heating Limitation: induction or water-bath only; open flame and resistive spark-generating elements are prohibited<br>
    - Vapor Control: extraction must occur in ventilated space or fume-controlled environment to prevent vapor accumulation
  </p>

  <p><strong>1.2 Biological Safety (Aspergillus oryzae)</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Exposure Risk: generally safe (GRAS), but airborne spores may cause respiratory irritation<br>
    - PPE Requirement: N95 respirator required during inoculation handling<br>
    - Sterilization Constraint: substrate must undergo 100°C / 30 min sterilization cycle<br>
    - Contamination Risk: failure to sterilize may permit growth of wild Aspergillus species with potential aflatoxin production
  </p>

  <p><strong>2. MATERIAL STABILITY & PERMANENCE</strong></p>

  <p><strong>2.1 Lightfastness Audit</strong></p>
  <p>
    - UV Sensitivity: betalains and anthocyanins exhibit photodegradation under direct light exposure<br>
    - Stabilization Protocols:<br>
    &nbsp;&nbsp;• Maintain aqueous pigment pH at ~4.5 using citric acid for chromophore stability<br>
    &nbsp;&nbsp;• Apply UV-absorbent varnish (MSA + UVLS or equivalent) for finished works<br>
    - Structural Mitigation: micronized kaolin may be used as a scattering matrix to reduce UV penetration in stick formulations
  </p>

  <p><strong>2.2 Microbial Integrity (Chitosan Shield)</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Risk: high-moisture pigment systems are susceptible to microbial growth<br>
    - Mitigation: integrate 0.1% chitosan solution by weight into finished concentrates<br>
    - Function: antimicrobial barrier and secondary binder interface with Lithic Substrate (Node 03)
  </p>

  <p><strong>3. ARCHIVAL STORAGE STANDARDS</strong></p>

  <p>
    Long-term pigment stability requires controlled environmental storage conditions.
  </p>

  <p>
    - Atmosphere: anhydrous, zero headspace environment<br>
    - Temperature: 5–20°C (41–68°F)<br>
    - Container: amber glass or aluminum to block UV exposure<br>
    - Seal: parafilm or induction seal to prevent oxidation and solvent loss (“skinning”)
  </p>

  <p><strong>4. THE FORENSIC SIGNATURE</strong></p>

  <p>
    A pigment batch is classified as “Stable” and “Audit-Ready” only if it passes the centrifuge stability threshold.
  </p>

  <p>
    - Centrifuge Test: 10 ml sample spun at 3000 RPM for 10 minutes<br>
    - Acceptance Criterion: no visible separation between pigment phase and binder matrix
  </p>
