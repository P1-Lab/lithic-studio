    <h1>Node 03: The Body (Botanical Substrate)</h1>
    <p><strong>Industrial Classification:</strong> LITH-BODY-03</p>
    <p><strong>Status:</strong> Forensic Specification / Stand-Alone Medium</p>
    <p><strong>Core Engine:</strong> Bio-Softened Cellulose &amp; Chitosan Structural Sizing</p>
  </header>

  <hr>

  <article>
    <h2>Overview</h2>
    <p>
      <strong>Node 03: The Body</strong> is a protocol for the fabrication of high-tensile, archival-grade botanical substrates. 
      This system utilizes a combination of <strong>Bio-Softened agricultural fiber</strong> (Palm, Flax, Hemp, or Grape Pomace) 
      and a <strong>Chitosan structural sizing</strong> to produce a medium that bridges the gap between traditional paper 
      and textile-polymer composites.
    </p>

    <p>
      While engineered to withstand the extreme mechanical loads of the <strong>Lithic Fresco (Node 02)</strong>, it is a 
      <strong>fully stand-alone medium</strong> optimized for high-end printmaking, watercolor, and ink-stick applications.
    </p>
  </article>

  <hr>

  <article>
    <h2>Project Scope</h2>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Enzymatic Fiber Liberation:</strong> Protocols for using Aspergillus oryzae to degrade lignocellulosic bonds, replacing harsh industrial caustic boils with biological “softening.”</li>
      <li><strong>Structural Sizing (The Chitosan Bridge):</strong> Application of Chitosan to create a liquid-proof, high-tooth surface that prevents pigment “sinking.”</li>
      <li><strong>Multi-Gauge Fabrication:</strong> Techniques for casting ultra-thin vellum through to 300gsm forensic boards.</li>
      <li><strong>Rigid Mounting:</strong> Technical ramps for bonding substrate to wood or aluminum for industrial-scale formats.</li>
    </ul>
  </article>

  <hr>

  <article>
    <h2>Technical Features</h2>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Mechanical Permanence:</strong> Preserved fiber length increases burst strength and fold endurance.</li>
      <li><strong>Forensic Surface:</strong> Chitosan sizing ensures Node 01 pigments remain surface-bound for maximum chromatic saturation.</li>
      <li><strong>Modular Integration:</strong> Chemically synchronized with Lithic Fresco to prevent oil migration (haloing) while maintaining permanent mechanical bond.</li>
    </ul>
  </article>

  <hr>

  <article>
    <h2>System Architecture</h2>
    <p>Node 03 is the foundational “Body” of the Lithic Studio ecosystem:</p>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Node 01 (The Light):</strong> Provides chromatic data applied to the surface.</li>
      <li><strong>Node 02 (The Form):</strong> Provides structural impasto built upon the surface.</li>
      <li><strong>Node 03 (The Body):</strong> The sovereign platform that hosts the event.</li>
    </ul>
