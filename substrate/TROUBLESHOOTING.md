  <h1># TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE: Node 03 (The Body)</h1>

  <p><strong>Industrial Classification:</strong> LITH-BODY-TS-03<br/>
  <strong>Status:</strong> VERIFIED<br/>
  <strong>Scope:</strong> Pulp Processing, Sheet Formation, and Sizing Integrity</p>

  <hr/>

  <h2>1. PULP &amp; FIBER FAILURES</h2>

  <h3>1.1 Problem: "Clumping" or "Knots" in the Sheet</h3>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Symptom:</strong> Small, hard nodules of fiber visible in the finished paper; uneven surface.</li>
    <li><strong>Root Cause:</strong> Insufficient beating time (Tier I or II) OR the fiber was not properly "teased" after the Bio-Softening phase.</li>
    <li><strong>Correction:</strong> Ensure the Aspergillus oryzae has fully penetrated the lignocellulosic bonds before milling.</li>
    <li><strong>Recovery:</strong> Return the wet pulp to the beater/mortar and add 10% more water to increase dispersion.</li>
  </ul>

  <h3>1.2 Problem: The "Slime" Factor (Over-Beating)</h3>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Symptom:</strong> Water will not drain through the screen; the sheet feels like jelly and shrinks drastically when drying.</li>
    <li><strong>Root Cause:</strong> Fiber was beaten too long, causing excessive hydration of cellulose.</li>
    <li><strong>Correction:</strong> Reduce milling time by 15-minute increments until drainage improves.</li>
    <li><strong>Recovery:</strong> Mix over-beaten pulp with fresh "short-beat" fiber (1:1 ratio) to restore drainage channels.</li>
  </ul>

  <hr/>

  <h2>2. FORMATION &amp; PRESSING FAILURES</h2>

  <h3>2.1 Problem: Thin Spots or "Windows"</h3>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Symptom:</strong> Inconsistent opacity; thin areas in center or edges.</li>
    <li><strong>Root Cause:</strong> Improper Okanagan Shake technique or premature lifting of mold from vat.</li>
    <li><strong>Correction:</strong> Maintain steady level pull; continue horizontal agitation until drainage stabilizes.</li>
    <li><strong>Recovery:</strong> Reintroduce sheet into vat briefly to redistribute fiber, then re-pull mold.</li>
  </ul>

  <h3>2.2 Problem: Delamination of Plies (Couching Failure)</h3>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Symptom:</strong> Sheet splits into layers when handled.</li>
    <li><strong>Root Cause:</strong> Excessive pressing force or partial drying before couching.</li>
    <li><strong>Correction:</strong> Keep post fully wet until hydraulic pressing stage.</li>
    <li><strong>Recovery:</strong> Re-press immediately under controlled staged pressure.</li>
  </ul>

  <hr/>

  <h2>3. SIZING &amp; SURFACE FAILURES (THE CHITOSAN AUDIT)</h2>

  <h3>3.1 Problem: Pigment "Feathering" or Bleeding</h3>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Symptom:</strong> Ink spreads into fibers; loss of edge definition.</li>
    <li><strong>Root Cause:</strong> Weak Chitosan sizing or absorption into sheet core.</li>
    <li><strong>Correction:</strong> Increase Chitosan concentration to 1.5% or apply to fully dry sheet.</li>
    <li><strong>Recovery:</strong> Apply second flash-coat of 1.0% Chitosan; cure 24 hours.</li>
  </ul>

  <h3>3.2 Problem: The Sheet is Brittle or "Snaps"</h3>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Symptom:</strong> Paper cracks under bending stress.</li>
    <li><strong>Root Cause:</strong> Over-sizing or lack of humectant in solution.</li>
    <li><strong>Correction:</strong> Add 2% glycerin to Chitosan solution.</li>
    <li><strong>Recovery:</strong> Light misting with 5% glycerin-water solution to restore flexibility.</li>
  </ul>

  <hr/>

  <h2>4. DRYING FAILURES</h2>

  <h3>4.1 Problem: Severe Warping or "Potato-Chipping"</h3>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Symptom:</strong> Sheet edges curl; surface loses flatness.</li>
    <li><strong>Root Cause:</strong> Rapid drying or lack of rigid mounting.</li>
    <li><strong>Correction:</strong> Use board-drying technique on sealed flat surface.</li>
    <li><strong>Recovery:</strong> Re-dampen, press between felts, and weight for 48 hours.</li>
  </ul>

  <hr/>

  <p><strong>A=A.</strong> The failure of the body is a failure of the ramp.<br/>
