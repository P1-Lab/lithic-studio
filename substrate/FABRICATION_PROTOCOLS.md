<h1>FABRICATION PROTOCOLS: Node 03 (The Body)</h1>

<p><b>Industrial Classification:</b> LITH-BODY-PROT-03</p>
<p><b>Status:</b> VERIFIED</p>
<p><b>Process:</b> Hand-Casting & Structural Curing</p>

<h2>1. VAT PREPARATION & DISPERSION</h2>

<p>The quality of the "Body" is determined by the homogeneity of the fiber suspension.</p>

<p>Hydration: Transfer bio-softened, beaten pulp into casting vat.</p>
<p>Ratio: 2% fiber-to-water for 300gsm sheets. 0.5% for 90gsm vellum.</p>
<p>Dispersion: Maintain low-shear agitation. No clumping or fiber knots permitted.</p>

<h2>2. SHEET FORMATION (THE CAST)</h2>

<h3>2.1 Mold & Deckle</h3>

<p>Screen Spec: 40-mesh stainless steel or heavy-duty silk.</p>

<p>Technique:</p>
<p>1. Submerge mold vertically into vat.</p>
<p>2. Rotate to horizontal and lift in a single motion.</p>
<p>3. Execute “Okanagan Shake” (5–10 seconds) to interlock fibers on X/Y axis.</p>

<h2>3. COUCHING & PRESSING</h2>

<h3>3.1 Transfer (Couching)</h3>

<p>Remove deckle frame.</p>
<p>Flip mold onto damp wool felt or recycled cotton carrier.</p>
<p>Apply pressure and lift screen; sheet transfers to felt.</p>

<h3>3.2 Compression Ramp</h3>

<p>Tier I (Lab):</p>
<p>Stage 1: 500 PSI for 5 minutes.</p>
<p>Stage 2: 2000 PSI for 10 minutes.</p>

<p>Tier II (Field):</p>
<p>Stack between rigid boards and apply ~100kg load or screw press.</p>

<h2>4. DRYING & SIZING RAMP</h2>

<h3>4.1 Tension Drying</h3>

<p>Sheets must dry under constraint to prevent warp.</p>

<p>Method: Board drying onto sealed wood or glass substrate.</p>
<p>Result: Shrinkage creates tensioned, planar surface.</p>

<h3>4.2 Chitosan Bridge Application</h3>

<p>Only after full drying:</p>

<p>Apply 1.0% Chitosan solution in cross-directional brush passes.</p>
<p>Allow 24-hour cure for acetic evaporation and crystallization.</p>

<p>Substrate is now chemically locked for Node 01 or Node 02 integration.</p>

<h2>5. POST-FABRICATION AUDIT</h2>

<p>Surface Integrity Test:</p>

<p>Apply diluted Node 01 pigment to 2x2 inch test zone.</p>

<p>PASS: Sharp edge retention, no feathering.</p>
<p>FAIL: Feathering indicates insufficient sizing; re-coat required.</p>

<p><b>A=A.</b> The protocol is the proof of the sheet.</p>
