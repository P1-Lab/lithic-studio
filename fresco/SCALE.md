  <p><strong>1. THE PHYSICS OF SCALE</strong></p>

  <p>
    Traditional impasto is limited by internal drying stress generated during oxidative polymerization of walnut oil matrices.
    On small formats, substrates absorb this stress; on large formats, it manifests as cracking or delamination.
  </p>

  <p>
    <strong>Micro-Lattice Advantage</strong><br>
    The Palm Fiber reinforcement (0.5–2cm) functions as a structural lattice distributing tensile stress throughout the media.
    This prevents localized stress concentration and enables continuous high-relief application up to ~20mm depth without failure.
  </p>

  <p><strong>2. SUBSTRATE ARCHITECTURE: RIGID MOUNT</strong></p>

  <p>
    For works exceeding 30 inches, Node 03 substrate transitions to rigid mounting to eliminate substrate flex.
  </p>

  <p>
    <strong>Bonding Protocol</strong><br>
    - Support: kiln-dried birch plywood or aluminum honeycomb panel<br>
    - Adhesive: 1.5% chitosan solution + 5% wheat starch paste<br>
    - Action: laminate wet substrate to rigid support using vacuum press or weighted rollers<br>
    - Cure: 48 hours drying prior to fresco application
  </p>

  <p><strong>3. TEXTURAL COMPARISON: LITHIC VS ENCAUSTIC</strong></p>

  <p>
    Lithic Fresco behaves as a fiber-reinforced oil composite rather than a thermally sensitive wax system.
  </p>

  <p>
    Mechanical Bond: fiber-to-fiber integration vs surface adhesion<br>
    Thermal Stability: thermoset (~200°C) vs wax melt (~65°C)<br>
    Mass: medium-light botanical composite vs dense wax mass<br>
    Surface Behavior: high-friction structural drag vs smooth slip
  </p>

  <p><strong>4. LARGE-SCALE APPLICATION LOGIC</strong></p>

  <p>
    <strong>Zonal Cure Protocol</strong><br>
    Large works develop oxygen gradients during early polymerization stages.
  </p>

  <p>
    - Maintain forced air circulation for first 14 days<br>
    - Cure horizontally for first 7 days to prevent gravitational creep<br>
    - Avoid vertical orientation until surface set stabilizes
  </p>

  <p>
    <strong>Material Estimate (10mm relief)</strong><br>
    Approx. 1.2 kg of media per square foot of coverage.<br>
    Continuous wet-on-wet application required for structural continuity.
  </p>

  <p><strong>5. STRATEGIC POSITIONING</strong></p>

  <p>
    Scaling Node 02 transitions the system into architectural-grade surface production.
    Applications include high-UV environments, institutional interiors, and large-scale public installations requiring long-term thermal and structural stability.
  </p>
