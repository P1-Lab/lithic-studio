  <p><strong>1. LIGNOCELLULOSIC ADDITIVES (THE REINFORCEMENT)</strong></p>
  <p>
    The structural integrity of the fresco media is dependent on the precise processing of regional botanical waste.
  </p>

  <p><strong>1.1 Palm Fiber Specifications (Structural Lattice)</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Source: Fallen tropical palm fronds<br>
    - Processing Protocol: 180-minute aqueous boil to remove water-soluble sugars and hemicellulose<br>
    - Dehydration Requirement: &lt;5% residual moisture to prevent oxidative rancidity in walnut oil matrix<br>
    - Mechanical Dimension: shredded to 0.5–2.0 cm lengths<br>
    - Function: micro-lattice reinforcement for high-volume impasto and delamination resistance
  </p>

  <p><strong>1.2 Citrus Pomace Specifications (The Aggregate)</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Source: Dehydrated citrus juice byproduct<br>
    - Granulometry: 500–1500 μm coarse grit<br>
    - Function: provides mechanical drag during application and increases volume solids without proportional weight increase
  </p>

  <p><strong>2. THE BINDING MATRIX (WALNUT OIL)</strong></p>

  <p><strong>2.1 Oil Metrics</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Refinement: 100% pure refined walnut oil<br>
    - Iodine Value: 140–150 (supports rapid oxidative polymerization of high-solids layers)<br>
    - Purity: anhydrous; moisture content must remain &lt;0.5% to maintain thixotropic stability
  </p>

  <p><strong>2.2 Biological Cohesion (Nori Paste)</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Extraction: 5g dry <em>Porphyra</em> rehydrated in 50g distilled water<br>
    - State: blended into homogeneous gelatinous mucilage<br>
    - Function: surfactant interface enabling bonding between aqueous-extracted pigments (Node 01) and anhydrous oil matrix
  </p>

  <p><strong>3. THIXOTROPIC STANDARDS (MECHANICAL INTEGRITY)</strong></p>

  <p>
    The Form must exhibit non-Newtonian behavior: stable under gravity while responsive under shear forces (palette knife application).
  </p>

  <p><strong>3.1 Stiff Peak Test (Forensic Standard)</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Protocol: 2 cm diameter media column applied to vertical substrate<br>
    - Requirement: maintain 1.5 cm vertical peak without collapse or sagging for ≥60 minutes<br>
    - Adjustment: introduce fumed silica in 0.5% increments by weight until threshold stability is achieved
  </p>

  <p><strong>4. CURING & ENVIRONMENTAL CONSTRAINTS</strong></p>

  <p><strong>4.1 Polymerization Timeline</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Surface Dry (tack-free): 7–10 days<br>
    - Through-Cure (≥5 mm): minimum 60 days<br>
    - Full Stabilization: 6 months
  </p>

  <p><strong>4.2 Storage Parameters</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Containment: airtight collapsible aluminum tubes or glass jars with zero headspace<br>
    - Temperature: 15–25°C<br>
    - Constraint: avoid direct UV exposure during storage phase
  </p>
