  <p><strong>1. THE OXIDATIVE CURE MECHANISM</strong></p>

  <p>
    Unlike acrylic or water-based systems that cure through evaporation, the Lithic Fresco Media cures via oxidative polymerization. The walnut oil matrix absorbs atmospheric oxygen and transitions into a cross-linked polymer network (linoxin).
  </p>

  <p><strong>1.1 The Through-Cure Timeline</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Stage 1 (0–48 Hours): Autoxidation phase. Oxygen absorption begins; no visible viscosity change<br>
    - Stage 2 (3–10 Days): Surface set. Exterior becomes tack-free while internal matrix remains mobile<br>
    - Stage 3 (10–60 Days): Cross-linking / through-cure. Full structural stabilization for layers >5 mm requires ~60 days<br>
    - Stage 4 (6–12 Months): Maturation phase. Maximum hardness, refractive stability, and long-term structural equilibrium
  </p>

  <p><strong>2. SURFACE INTERACTION & ANCHORING</strong></p>

  <p>
    The Form is engineered for direct chemical and mechanical integration with the Lithic Substrate (Node 03).
  </p>

  <p><strong>2.1 The Chitosan Bridge</strong></p>
  <p>
    The substrate’s chitosan sizing creates a micro-textured interface. Upon application, the oxidative oil matrix mechanically anchors into these pores, forming a permanent bond that resists separation and oil migration. This prevents “haloing” while maintaining structural adhesion.
  </p>

  <p><strong>2.2 Sinking & Refractive Index Behavior</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Sinking: Under-sized substrate absorbs excess oil, reducing chromatic intensity and producing a matte, under-bound surface<br>
    - Correction: Apply a controlled “oiling-out” layer of refined walnut oil to restore surface saturation and optical balance
  </p>

  <p><strong>3. FORENSIC STABILITY AUDIT</strong></p>

  <p>
    The following environmental and procedural constraints are required to ensure long-term structural integrity.
  </p>

  <p>
    <strong>Constraints & Risk Controls</strong><br><br>

    - Premature Varnishing → risk of cracking or delamination<br>
    Mitigation: no varnish application before 6 months of cure time<br><br>

    - Low Airflow → delayed oxidation and persistent tackiness<br>
    Mitigation: maintain continuous ambient air circulation; avoid sealed curing environments<br><br>

    - High Humidity → hydrolytic weakening of oil polymer network<br>
    Mitigation: maintain relative humidity below 60% during active cure phase<br><br>

    - Substrate Flex → mechanical fracture of thick impasto layers<br>
    Mitigation: use only high-tensile Lithic Substrate systems
  </p>

  <p><strong>4. POST-CURE CHARACTERISTICS</strong></p>

  <p>
    - Yellowing: minimized through selection of walnut oil (lower linolenic acid content than linseed oil)<br>
    - Flexibility: retained micro-flex due to embedded palm fiber lattice, reducing brittleness in long-term aging
  </p>
