  <p><strong>COMPOUNDING PROTOCOLS: Node 02 (The Form)</strong></p>

  <p>
    <strong>Industrial Classification:</strong> LITH-FORM-PROT-02<br>
    <strong>Status:</strong> VERIFIED<br>
    <strong>Process Type:</strong> Low-Shear Mechanical Compounding
  </p>

  <p><strong>1. PRE-COMPUTATION & EQUIPMENT</strong></p>
  <p>
    Prior to compounding, verify all additives meet moisture content and granulometry thresholds defined in TECHNICAL_SPECIFICATIONS.md.
  </p>

  <p><strong>1.1 Required Equipment</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Mixing Surface: non-porous glass slab with muller or HDPE vessel<br>
    - Agitation Tools: stainless steel palette knives or low-RPM horizontal mixer (industrial scale)<br>
    - Measurement: digital scale with 0.1g resolution<br>
    - Environment: &lt;50% relative humidity to prevent unintended moisture uptake
  </p>

  <p><strong>2. THE COMPOUNDING SEQUENCE</strong></p>

  <p><strong>STAGE 1: Pigment Dispersion (The Chromatic Base)</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Ratio: 100g anhydrous pigment paste (Node 01) to 200g refined walnut oil<br>
    - Method: incrementally add oil in 50g steps while mulling in figure-eight motion<br>
    - Target State: homogeneous high-viscosity dispersion with zero visible agglomeration
  </p>

  <p><strong>STAGE 2: Fiber & Aggregate Integration (The Matrix)</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Additives:<br>
    &nbsp;&nbsp;• 150g citrus pomace (500–1500 μm)<br>
    &nbsp;&nbsp;• 50g processed palm fiber (0.5–2 cm)<br>
    &nbsp;&nbsp;• 50g nori cohesion paste (biological surfactant)<br><br>

    - Method: fold additives into pigment base using low-shear manual or paddle mixing<br>
    - Constraint: DO NOT use high-shear mixing. Excess RPM will destroy fiber structure and eliminate mechanical drag properties<br>
    - Objective: full wet-out of fibers without fragmentation
  </p>

  <p><strong>STAGE 3: Final Viscosity Adjustment</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Add: 200g refined walnut oil (final dilution phase)<br>
    - Result: transition from dry particulate matrix to cohesive viscous paste<br>
    - Thixotropic Control: if collapse occurs under load, add fumed silica in 5g increments until structural stability is achieved (see Stiff Peak Test)
  </p>

  <p><strong>3. PACKAGING & VOID ELIMINATION</strong></p>
  <p>
    - Transfer: immediately seal into airtight containers post-compounding<br>
    - De-aeration: mechanically tap container to release trapped air and reduce oxidative void formation<br>
    - Oxygen Barrier: apply thin surface layer of walnut oil prior to sealing for oxidation control
  </p>

  <p><strong>4. BATCH VERIFICATION</strong></p>

  <p>A batch is classified as “Forensic Grade” only if all criteria are met:</p>
  <p>
    - Separation Test: no oil bleed after 24-hour rest period<br>
    - Fiber Distribution: uniform dispersion of palm fiber throughout matrix<br>
    - Structural Test: 1.5 cm vertical peak retention maintained for ≥60 minutes
  </p>
