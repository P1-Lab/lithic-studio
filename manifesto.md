  <p><strong>MANIFESTO: THE LITHIC STUDIO</strong></p>

  <p><strong>I. THE CORE THESIS</strong></p>
  <p>
    The modern creative economy is built on a “Chemical Tax”—a reliance on synthetic resins, petroleum-derived binders, and global supply chains that prioritize shelf-stability over material integrity. <strong>The Lithic Studio</strong> is a clinical rejection of this model.
  </p>

  <p>
    We propose a <strong>Mono-Material Ecosystem</strong> where the tools of expression are derived from the same biological engine as the life of the creator.
  </p>

  <p><strong>II. MATERIAL INTEGRITY (A=A)</strong></p>
  <p>
    In this laboratory, the artwork is not a collection of disparate industrial chemicals. It is a unified biological event.
  </p>

  <p>
    - The Engine: <em>Aspergillus oryzae</em> (Koji) is used as a molecular scalpel to liberate pigments and soften recalcitrant fibers.<br>
    - The Body: Fallen palm fronds and botanical waste serve as structural substrate (paper) and physical drag (fresco) of the work.<br>
    - The Light: Spices and regional flora provide chromatic data extracted through controlled thermal and solvent processes rather than synthetic dyes.
  </p>

  <p><strong>III. DETERMINISTIC REPLICATION</strong></p>
  <p>
    The system rejects ambiguity in craft. All protocols are defined as forensic procedural blueprints.
  </p>

  <p>
    - Fermentation is executed at 82°F (28°C) for 72 hours<br>
    - Material composition is defined through weight-by-weight ratios and micron-scale filtration<br>
    - Outcome is not interpretive; it is physically constrained by process parameters
  </p>

  <p>
    If the physics are followed, the result is deterministic.
  </p>

  <p><strong>IV. THE CLOSED LOOP</strong></p>
  <p>
    The Lithic Studio operates on a closed biological cycle:
  </p>

  <p>
    1. Input: Regional botanical waste and fungal cultures<br>
    2. Process: Biological fermentation and mechanical fibrillation<br>
    3. Output: Professional-grade art media and substrates<br>
    4. Residue: Compostable biological matter returned to soil systems
  </p>

  <p><strong>V. THE SOVEREIGN OBJECT</strong></p>
  <p>
    By controlling the vertical stack—from fiber formation to pigment extraction—the artist achieves material sovereignty. The resulting work is independent of industrial supply chains: permanent, non-toxic, and forensically traceable.
  </p>
